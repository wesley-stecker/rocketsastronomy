Astronomy is the study of objects and phenomena beyond Earth's atmosphere that we can see in the sky. With just our eyes, we can observe:

The Sun: The bright star at the center of our solar system, providing light and heat. Please do not look directley at the sun it can and will damage your eyes.

The Moon: Earth's natural satellite, visible in different phases as it orbits Earth.

Stars: Tiny points of light scattered across the night sky. Some appear brighter or different colors.

Planets: Certain stars that don't twinkle and move relative to the background stars over weeks and months. These include Venus, Mars, Jupiter, and Saturn.

Constellations: Patterns of stars that form recognizable shapes, like Orion or the Big Dipper.

Meteors: Streaks of light from small particles burning up as they enter Earth's atmosphere, commonly known as "shooting stars."

Comets: Occasionally visible objects with glowing heads and long, trailing tails.

By observing these objects and their movements, ancient astronomers laid the groundwork for our understanding of the cosmos.
